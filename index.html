As a second-year political science major concentrating in International Affairs at the University of California, Riverside, I am committed to fostering justice and equity, particularly in the field of immigration law. From a young age, I have been passionate about helping marginalized communities, driven by my personal experiences growing up in a household with undocumented parents. My desire to advocate for immigrant rights is not only a career goal but a deeply rooted mission inspired by my firsthand understanding of the complexities and struggles faced by undocumented individuals. At UC Riverside, I am actively involved in both academic and extracurricular activities that sharpen my leadership, critical thinking, and legal skills. I am a dedicated team member of our Mock Trial team, where I enhance my understanding of legal processes, argumentation, and public speaking. This experience allows me to apply critical thinking, a skill I am further developing through my coursework in Philosophy and Critical Thinking.

In addition to Mock Trial, I am the City Council Coordinator on the Legislative Acts Committee, a role in which I collaborate with local government bodies to develop policies that address community needs. This position has expanded my understanding of legislative processes and honed my ability to navigate complex political systems. My work on this committee strengthens my leadership abilities, communication skills, and passion for civic engagement, all of which are essential for my future in law. In high school, I demonstrated my leadership potential by serving as the President of the Leadership Club, where I organized community events and mentored peers. I was also a Link Crew mentor, assisting younger students with their academic and personal transitions to high school. These early experiences fostered my ability to connect with diverse groups and develop meaningful relationships, skills I continue to refine in my university life.

Fluent in both English and Spanish and a recipient of the Seal of Biliteracy, I am committed to using my bilingual skills to support and advocate for immigrants in both languages. I believe that effective communication is key to building trust with clients, especially those facing the vulnerabilities associated with immigration issues. Currently, I am looking to expand my network and gain more hands-on experience through internships, fellowships, and additional leadership roles. My goal is to graduate in 2027 and pursue law school, where I plan to specialize in immigration law, continuing my advocacy for immigrant rights and social justice.
